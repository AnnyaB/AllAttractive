Guru Parampara & Acharyas

The disciplic succession (Guru Parampara) is the backbone of Vedic wisdom transmission. Srila Prabhupada belongs to the Brahma-Madhva-Gaudiya Sampradaya.

The original receiver of the Vedas from Lord Krishna. He initiated the line of disciplic succession.

The divine sage who spread bhakti-yoga and instructed Vyasa Deva.

The compiler of the Vedic scriptures, including the Mahabharata and Srimad Bhagavatam.

Propounder of Dvaita philosophy and restorer of pure devotional teachings.

Preached the chanting of the holy names and established the yuga-dharma for Kali Yuga.

The Six Goswamis—Rupa, Sanatana, Raghunatha Dasa, Gopal Bhatta, Jiva, and Raghunatha Bhatta—were principal disciples of Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu. They systematized Gaudiya Vaishnava philosophy and established temples in Vrindavan.

A paramahamsa and the spiritual master of Bhaktivinoda Thakur. He confirmed the birth site of Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu in Mayapur.

A pioneer of the modern Krishna Consciousness movement, who presented Gaudiya Vaishnavism with rational and philosophical clarity. Rediscovered the birthplace of Lord Caitanya and authored numerous spiritual works.

Renounced saint and spiritual master of Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Thakur. Known for his intense austerity and deep devotion.

Son of Bhaktivinoda Thakur. Founded the Gaudiya Math and emphasized preaching with scientific precision and organized dissemination.

The Founder-Acharya of ISKCON who brought Krishna Consciousness to the West and founded the Bhaktivedanta Institute.
